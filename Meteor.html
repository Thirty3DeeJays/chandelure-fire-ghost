Meteor

Professional Valorant's Best Salaryman

While it's important to look at and recognize the GOATS, it is equally important to look at the average man. The day trader, the salaryman, the 9-5'er. While Meteor is no slouch, he definitely isn't the biggest name on this list. While he still is the salaryman, he definitely has his moments in the spotlight. Kim "Meteor" Tae-o is a Korean player who is currently playing for Gen.G Esports.

9 AM

Meteor is a remarkable player, don't get me wrong. His aim and mechanics are off the charts, and he is easily one of the most refined players in all of VCT. It's just, in a game where a Korean girl can glide, a British man can sent himself on fire, and a Chinese woman can make a giant block wall of ice, you're going to have to do a bit more to stand out than just "have good aim". Meteor "clocked in" to Valorant in 2021, playing for TNL Esports. He was good on TNL, but not the best on his team. The best word for Meteor at this point would be "grinder".

11:30 (You thought it was later)

In 2022, Meteor was picked up by Northeption, a Japanese ESports team. Northeption would actually go on to qualify for a Masters event that year, Master Copenhagen. They didn't place well, finishing 9th-10th out of the 12 teams that were at the event. Meteor played decent in the 3 matches he and his team played, being eliminated by the eventual champions of the tournament, FunPlusPhoenix. It seemed like Meteor would be another stat on the scoresheet.

Getting called into your bosses office

Meteor left Northeption in October of 2022 to join GenG. And this was the glow up he needed. It was like getting a promotion from your boss, to play for a better team. Meteor played more confidently, and with more swagger and- oh what's that? GenG didn't make a single international event in 2023? Oh, so yeah Meteor didn't make it to any international events in 2023. Since there was the LOCK-IN tournament, which GenG was not at, and then GenG missed out on qualifying for Masters Tokyo, and then they failed to qualify through the LCQ (Last Chance Qualifier) and effecively, their season was over. But then, something changed.

5 PM/Promotion

GenG signed 3 new players to their squad. Meteor was still playing, but with this new squad, they seemed to be unstopable. GenG would still be second to Paper Rex in the Pacific league, but as long as you make the international tournament, who cares what you placed beforehand. Meteor got a promotion, and now, he could play in international games and show the world who he truly was. In fact, at Masters Madrid, GenG reached the Grand Finals, where they lost to Sentinels, but secured second place. However, while making this project, another Masters event happened, Masters Shanghai, where, you guessed it, GenG ended up winning the whole event. In fact, it is the first trophy an APAC team has ever won at an international event. And guess who got to hold that trophy? Our best salaryman, Meteor.
