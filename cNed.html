cNed

Professional Valorant's Guy

When doing research for this project, I saw that cNed, like zeek was on Acend. CNed, unlike zeek, has seen success post Acend. Since I just went over Acend's journey, I won't here. But cNed, like Meteor, is kinda just a guy. Mehmet "cNed" Ä°pek is a Turkish player who is currently playing for FUT Esports.

Post Acend cNed

After winning Champs 2021, cNed stuck with Acend for the 2022 season. Like all duelists, he played Chamber during this season. But he had a down year. Only amassing 629 kills as a duelist nasty work. Acend didn't qualify for any international events, so naturally, cNed played less Valorant because of it. After the 2022 season, cNed decided to leave Acend. This is where he is picked up by a new team, and this is where cNed's story begins.

cNed on NAVI

cNed was signed by NAVI in November of 2022. He played really well in LOCK-IN, pulling NAVI into 3rd-4th. However, cNed was unable to make NAVI win EMEA. They did qualify for international events. Both Masters Tokyo and Champs 2023, however NAVI had a poor showing at both of these events and it lead to a decision being made about cNed.

FUT ESports/Going Home

cNed decided to leave NAVI in favor of joining FUT ESports. You would think he just moves around and doesn't really stay in one place. However, FUT is a Turkish ESports organization. cNed is Turkish, so it is kind of a homecoming for him. He has also qualified for both international events that have happened this year, so safe to say he's feeling the best he has during his career. Currently, he has a KDR of 1.32 with FUT, which is really good. A good KDR in my eyes is about 1, so to be that far over that is insanely cool.

Why just a guy?

I needed to put the average in. CNed, in my head, is an average duelist. He's good, not great. He has his bad and good moments. But that is what makes him interesting to me.
